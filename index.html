Captain MS Dhoni hitting the winning shot in WC 2011 final.

Here's is a timeline of MS Dhoni's International Cricket career:

  • 2004- Dhoni made his international debut against Bangladesh in an ODI at Chittagong
  • 2005-Dhoni makes his Test debut against Sri Lanka in Chennai.
  • 2007-Dhoni takes over the reins of ODI captaincy from Rahul Dravid. Dhoni also becomes captain of the Indian squad for the inaugural ICC World Twenty20 in South Africa.
  • 2008-Dhoni wins the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna award. Dhoni wins the ICC ODI Player of the Year honour.
  • 2009-Dhoni honoured with the Padma Shri, India's fourth highest civilian honour. Under Dhoni's captaincy, India tops the ICC Test cricket rankings for the first time. Dhoni retains his ICC ODI Player of the year award, becomes the first player to win it twice.
  • 2010- Dhoni leads Chennai Super Kings to their maiden Indian Premier League title.
  • 2011-Dhoni plays the iconic knock of unbeaten 91 off 79 balls against Sri Lanka in the World Cup final to help India lift the trophy after 28 years. Later, Chennai Super Kings retain the IPL, beat Royal Challengers Banglore by 58 runs in the final in Bengaluru. The Indian Territorial Army confers the honorary rank of Lieutenant Colonel on Dhoni.
  • 2013- Dhoni becomes the most successful Indian Test captain, eclipses Sourav Ganguly's record of 21 victories from 49 Tests. India wins the ICC Champions Trophy under Dhoni's captaincy, beats England by five runs in the final. Dhoni becomes the first captain to win all three ICC trophies.
  • 2018- Dhoni receives the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civillian honour.
  • 2018-Under Dhoni's captaincy, Chennai Super Kings win their third IPL title by defeating Sunrisers Hyderabad by eight wickets.
  • Aug 15, 2020-Dhoni finishs off in style.
